2. Paper Trading Results - Not Real Returns

All performance metrics displayed on this platform are based on PAPER TRADING (simulated trading) and NOT real money trading. Paper trading results have significant limitations and should not be relied upon as an indicator of future actual trading results.

Paper trading differs from real trading in several important ways:

  • No Real Execution: Paper trades don't interact with actual markets
  • Simulated Slippage: We estimate 0.15% slippage, but real slippage can be significantly higher
  • No Market Impact: Paper trading doesn't account for how your orders might move prices
  • Liquidity Assumptions: We assume trades can always be executed, which isn't always true in real markets
  • Emotional Factors: Paper trading eliminates the psychological aspects of trading with real money
  • Perfect Fills: Paper trading assumes orders are filled at expected prices, which rarely happens in practice

4. Risk Warnings

Trading securities involves substantial risk of loss. You should be aware of the following risks:

  • Market Risk: Stock prices can decline significantly, resulting in loss of principal
  • Liquidity Risk: Some securities may be difficult to buy or sell at desired prices

Only invest money you can afford to lose. Do not invest your emergency fund, retirement savings, or money needed for essential expenses.

9. Insider Trading Definition

For clarity, "insider trading" as discussed on this platform refers to LEGAL insider trading - that is, trades made by corporate insiders (officers, directors, and major shareholders) that are properly disclosed to the SEC via Form 4 filings within the required timeframe.

This platform does NOT encourage, facilitate, or provide information for ILLEGAL insider trading, which involves trading on material non-public information in violation of securities laws.
